FeedBot - A MessengerBot

Background

During Winter 2016, I worked at QuickTapSurvey and every 4 months, they host an inhouse hackathon for their employees to try and learn new tech and get a little break from the normal routine.

A few weeks prior to the hackathon, Facebook had their Developer Conference where the introduced Bots on their Messenger platform. I heard alot about these bots and even went to a tech meetup about Bots but I never messed around with one.

So, I decided to create my own messenger bot within the timeframe(~9 hrs) and see what I can come up with.

The idea to come up with this FeedBot was inspired from the fact that the company I was working for (QuickTapSurvey) provided a survey creation platform and so I wanted to use this MessengerBot to be able to collect information from the users as if I was taking a survey and then analyze it.


What is FeedBot?

FeedBot is a MessengerBot that collects people's opinions on current trends occurring in their area and analyzes that information

The strength of MessengerBot is that we can present the user with choices and users can click on a button and based on their choice, we can perform some action. This allowed me to present the user with a few trending topics in their location and allow them to click on the trend instead of typing it out. After I collected people's opinions on a trending topic, it was time to analyze it. With the help of IBM's AlchemyLanguageAPI, it analyzed my content to spit out an sentiment analysis of how people felt about a certain trend. Along with the sentiment analysis, I decided to use Google Charts to show the responses people had and used FacebookAPI to automatically post the graph to FeedBot's Facebook Page.


Tech and API's

NodeJS, FacebookAPI, GoogleTrendsAPI, AlchemyLanguageAPI, Firebase

SlackTranslate - A Slack Command

Background

During my coop term at QuickTapSurvey, we support 4 languages; EN, ES, FR, PT. That means, we have to localize a word or phrase into four different languages. This means, going on GoogleTranslate and typing the word or pharse and selecting the languages you want to translate to 3 times.

I noticed this and I wondered if there was a way to make this process easier and much faster. Since we used Slack, I came up with the idea of using a Slack command to fetch the translations. SlackTranslate was born.

SlackTranslate is a slack command which takes in a word or phrase and spits out the translations in French, Spanish and Portuguese.


Features:

- SlackTranslate first looks at our local db of localization and determines if the word the user is trying to translate is already in our db.

- If there is no translation in our local db, I use an API to get the translations and send that back


Tech and API's

NodeJS, SlackAPI, MicrosoftTranslateAPI
