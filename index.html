Baraat Swaagat & Pokhwanu


The Grooms's Entry With A Fun-Filled Procession

The groom and his family and friend will dance their way to the wedding (Baraat procession), showing off their moves and will be greeted by the bride’s family and friends.

The bride’s mother welcomes the group, and asks him if he is prepared to make the commitment of marriage to her daughter. The bride’s mother places kumkum (red vermilion powder), signifying good luck, on the groom’s forehead.

The groom is then escorted to the mandap where he awaits the bride’s arrival.

Jai Mala


The Exchange Of Garlands

The couple greet each by exchanging flower garlands, signifying their acceptance of each other as lifelong partners. BFFs forever! Awww...

Ganesh Puja


Prayers To Lord Ganesh

The ceremony begins with a prayer honouring Lord Ganesh, the remover of all obstacles. As per Hindu mythology, Lord Ganesh is always worshipped before starting any new chapter in one's life!

Var Puja


The Honouring Of The Groom

The groom is honoured by the bride's parents, who clean his feet with honey and milk. for his new life ahead with their daughter! How fancv! Isn't it?

SHHHHH... The bridesmaids will try to steal the groom's shoes once he is in the mandap His shoes will be held ransom unless the groom digs deep into his pocket to get them back!

Kanya Magman


The Bride's Entry

The bride is accompanied by her uncles / brothers as she enters the wedding, who will be holding a "phoolon ki chadar". As the bride enters the mandap, they are separated by an Antarpat (cloth) which signifies that they come as separate individuals.

Kandyadaan & Hasta Melap


Giving Away Of The Bride & The "Meeting Of Hands"

The brides parents, with the blessings of the Almighty, will place the bride's right hand in the groom's right hand thereby entrusting her happiness to him from now on. The groom's scarf is then tied to the bride's veil signifying the union of their souls. Happy Wife = Happy Life!

Agni Puja & Mangal Pheras


Four Circle Around The Holy Fire

The couple walk around the fire four times, symbolising the core aspirations of married life - Dharma (Duty & Religion), Artha (Prosperity), Kama (Family) and Moksha (Salvation).

Saptapadi


The Seven Vows Of Martial Bliss

The couple will take seven sacred steps & recite the following vows to one another:
1) To keep a pure household, avoiding all things harmful to our health.
2) To develop mental, physical & spiritual strength.
3) To acquire wealth by righteous means.
4) To acquire knowledge, happiness and harmony by mutual love and trust.
5) To pray for virtuous, intelligent and courageous children.
6) To exercise self-restraint for longevity.
7) To vow to always remain true companions and life-long partners.

Mangalsutra & Sindoor


Necklace & Powder of Promise

The mangalsutra necklace & sindoor (red powder on the bride's forehead) symbolises the bride's lifelong protection by the groom & his promise to fulfill her every wish.

Kansar Bhojan


The Couple's First Meal Together

The couple feed cach other sweets, symbolising that they will share everything they have in life.

Akhand Saubhagyavati


Blessings From Married Women

Married women whisper "Akhand Saubhagyavati" in the bride's ear, which means "good luck, prosperity and a long happy life".

Aashivaad & Vidaai


Blessings To The Couple & An Emotional Farewell

The priest now declares the couple "man & wife". The happy couple seck blessings from God, the priest, their parents & all of you! The bride & her family are teary eyed during this emotional goodbye!
